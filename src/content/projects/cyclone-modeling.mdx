# Cyclone Modeling using ODE/PDE

Cyclones are complex atmospheric phenomena driven by nonlinear interactions between pressure fields, temperature gradients, wind dynamics, and the Coriolis force.  
During my M1 Research Internship, I worked on a project focused on **numerically simulating cyclone behavior** using both **Ordinary Differential Equations (ODEs)** and **Partial Differential Equations (PDEs)**.

This work combined **mathematical modeling**, **numerical analysis**, and **scientific computing** to better understand the evolution of cyclone trajectories and their internal structure.

- Compare the behavior of different solvers  
- Analyze asymptotic behavior near the cyclone‚Äôs eye

## üßÆ Mathematical Formulation

Two types of equations were examined:

### **1. Ordinary Differential Equations (ODEs)**
ODEs were used to describe simplified trajectories and radial profiles of cyclone motion.

Typical governing equations involve:
- Radial velocity  
- Pressure gradients  
- Coriolis-like terms  
- Simplified thermodynamic relationships  

### **2. Partial Differential Equations (PDEs)**
To represent more detailed dynamics (e.g., wind fields, vorticity), PDEs were considered.

Examples include:
- Convection‚Äìdiffusion equations  
- Shallow-water-like approximations  
- Simplified Navier‚ÄìStokes-inspired models  

---

## üß™ Numerical Methods

To solve the ODEs and PDEs efficiently, I implemented several numerical techniques in Python.

### üîπ **Lax‚ÄìFriedrichs Scheme**  
Used for hyperbolic PDEs modeling convection.  
This scheme provides stability and is easy to implement, ideal for early prototyping of cyclone dynamics.

### üîπ **Implicit Euler & Œ∏-Method**  
Robust for stiff ODEs and equations representing dissipative processes.

### üîπ **BDF (Backward Differentiation Formula)**  
A high-accuracy solver suited for stiff dynamics, used to benchmark the simpler methods.

### üîπ **Cross-Method Validation**  
I developed a dedicated Python script to validate numerical solvers against each other:  
- Comparing trajectories  
- Measuring numerical error  
- Visualizing divergence between methods  
- Tracking stability regions

## üî¨ Asymptotic Analysis

A significant part of the project involved studying the **near-eye region**, where certain model parameters approach singular behavior.

This required analyzing:

- What happens as the function \( a(\xi) \to 0 \)  
- How the radial solution behaves in this limit  
- Numerical artifacts vs. true physical behavior  
- Conditions under which the models remain stable  

This provided insights into the limitations of simplified cyclone models and how to refine them.
